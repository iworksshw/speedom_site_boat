Let’s explore the fundamental information about boating race to enjoy its unique thrill and charm.

Thrilling races await spectators at the boating stadium. By learning basic information about boating race, you can enjoy it even more.

Race Process

After the starting signal, riders set off from the starting line and complete five laps (1,691 metres) on the track. A lead guide leads the race initially, reducing wind resistance for the riders. Once the lead guide exits the track, the race enters its critical stage. When the bell rings with one and a half laps remaining to the finish line, the final sprint begins in earnest.

The race concludes in approximately three minutes, delivering a high-intensity and captivating experience throughout its duration.

Pre-Race - Introduction Run

  • 01 Rider Confirmation

    Before the race, the racers perform high-speed practice runs on the racecourse, completing up to two laps at full speed to showcase their skill and the performance of their motorboats to the audience.

  • 02 Introduction Run Time Announcement

    During the introduction run, the time taken to travel the 150m distance from the backstretch centre to the second turn mark is measured during the first or second lap and provided to the audience.

  • 02-01 Online Start Run and Time Announcement

    To provide information about the racers' reaction speeds to the start signal and the performance of the motorboats, the time taken to cover the 335m distance from the dock to the flying start line is measured and shared with the audience.

Pre-Race - Ticket Sales

  • 03 Commencement of Betting on Winning Tickets

    Details are marked on the purchase slip, and race tickets are bought.

  • 04 Closure of Betting

    Once customer betting closes, the payout odds for each betting type are finalised and displayed on the digital board.

Race Progression

  • 05 Race Start (Departure from Pit Area)

    Once the large clock activates, six boats leave the pit area simultaneously at the start signal.

    In online start races, the race begins as the boats leave the pit area following the start signal.

  • 06 Standby Behaviour

    This refers to the movement of motorboats after departing from the pit area until they cross the start line. There are two systems in use: the course competition system or the fixed entry system.

  • 07 Start

    The race uses the flying start method, in which the motorboats cross the start line within 1.0 seconds while in motion.

  • 08 Race

    Once the large clock activates, six boats leave the pit area simultaneously at the start signal.

  • 09 Arrival

    This refers to the movement of motorboats after departing from the pit area until they cross the start line. There are two systems in use: the course competition system or the fixed entry system.

  • 10 Finish

    The race uses the flying start method, in which the motorboats cross the start line within 1.0 seconds while in motion.

End of Race

  • 11 Payout of Winngings

    Winngings are paid to customers who have winning betting tickets.

Unique Race Format in Boating

Departure from Pit Area and Standby Behaviour

The movement of the six boats from leaving the pit area until they cross the start line is referred to as standby behaviour. After departing the pit area following the start signal, racers compete to secure advantageous lanes under the course competition system, or move to their designated lanes without competition under the fixed lane entry system. Currently, the fixed lane entry system is being implemented.

Standby Run

It occurs between the departure from the pit area and the start of the entry run. There are six boats in numerical order in the pit area. In the fixed lane entry system, the number assigned at the pit area corresponds to the lane number, while in the course competition system, racers select their desired lane immediately after leaving the dock.

Course Competition System

It occurs between the departure from the pit area and the start of the entry run. There are six boats in numerical order in the pit area. In the fixed lane entry system, the number assigned at the pit area corresponds to the lane number, while in the course competition system, racers select their desired lane immediately after leaving the dock.

Entry Run

After completing the standby run, all boats head toward the start line. The movement until they cross the start line is called the entry run. The act of moving into the chosen or designated lane is referred to as “entry.”

The Flying Start: Key to Success

Boat racing does not use a stationary start like the online start method. Instead, the flying start method is employed, where boats in motion must cross the start line within a designated timeframe.
After leaving the pit area following the start signal, racers must cross the start line within 0 to 1.0 seconds of the starting time. If they cross the start line before the start time, they are subject to the Flying (premature start) rule. If they exceed 1.0 seconds after the start time, the Late start rule applies. In both cases, the racer is disqualified, and the affected customer's betting tickets are refunded.

Start Timing (ST)

Start timing is the value indicating how many seconds after the large clock's hand reaches 0 a boat's rear edge crosses the start line. For example, if the value is 0.11, it means the boat crossed the start line 0.11 seconds after 0 seconds.
