How to Read the Racecard
  • 01 Race Information
    It provides race times and starting schedules by time slot, along with details of race grades (rookie, standard) and types of races.
  • 02 Weight
    The athletes' weight is measured, and those below the minimum weight criteria (55kg for men, 51kg for women) are required to carry additional weight in order to participate in the race.
  • 03 Results Over the Last 6 Races
    Provides information on the average placing points, average score, winning percentage, top two percentage, top three percentage, and average ST/OST records for the last six races participated in.
  • 04 Placing in the Last 8 Races
    Shows the placing records from the last 8 races participated in, listed in order from the most recent performance on the left.
  • 05 Annual Cumulative Results
    Represents the annual cumulative average placing points and top two percentage.
  • 06 F/L
    Indicates the number of false starts during the grading evaluation period, recorded on a biannual basis.
  • 07 Average Incident Points
    Represents the average incident points recorded during the grading evaluation period.
  • 08 Today’s Other Races
    Lists the race numbers for today’s events, excluding the current race.
  • 09 Previous Day’s Results
    Provides details of the previous day’s races, including the placings and courses used.
  • 10 Top Two Percentage by Course/Number of Races Entered by Course Over 6 Months
    The athlete's top two percentage by course and number of races entered by course over 6 months
  • 11 Motor Performance
    Number, average placing points, top two percentage
  • 12 Boat Performance
    Number, average placing points, top two percentage
  • ?? Average Placing Points
    An objective measure of an athlete’s racing ability, calculated by dividing the total placing points (awarded based on race placings) by the number of races entered.
  • ?? Average Scores
    The score derived by subtracting the average incident points (total incident points ÷ number of races entered) from the average placing points (total placing points ÷ number of races entered)
  • ?? Winning Percentage (Top Two Percentage)ㆍ(Top Three Percentage)
    The number of races finished in 1st place (1st and 2nd place) (1st, 2nd, and 3rd place) divided by the number of races entered, expressed as a percentage
  • ?? Average ST (Start Time)
    A measure indicating the time it takes for a boat to cross the starting line during a flying start race after the large clock reaches 0 seconds. A lower number signifies a faster start
  • ?? Average OST (Online Start Time)
    In an online start race, this measure indicates the time taken to travel from the moment the starting signal is given and the starting mechanism is released to the moment the boat crosses the flying start line (a distance of 335m). A lower value represents a faster start
  • ?? Average Incident Points
    This score is the sum of the incident points during the corresponding grading evaluation period divided by the number of races entered

    Incident points are points deducted from a racer for disqualifications, warnings, or cautions during a race
