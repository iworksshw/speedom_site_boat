Boat racing is a competitive race based on ranking, rather than records, making it a type of interactive marine sport.

Boat racing is a thrilling leisure activity, featuring high-speed, action-packed races full of unpredictable results influenced by various factors.
From the starting dock to the finish line, spectators can enjoy the exhilarating spray and speed while predicting the winning racer.

Types of Boat Racing

General Race

Refers to boating races held every Wednesday and Thursday.

Grand Prix Boat Racing

Grand Prix Boat Racing features competitions with specific titles on the line, involving athletes with excellent performance over a set period.
Prizes such as trophies and other awards are given to athletes finishing in first, second, and third places.

Grand Prix Boat Racing

The final event of the season, featuring the best athletes of the year competing to be crowned the boat racing champion.

Special Boat Racing

Boating races organised for specific purposes. Notable examples include the Kurihara Race, the Queen's Cup, and the Rookie's Championship.  

Types of Races

Flying Start

Unique to boat racing, the flying start involves six motorboats crossing an imaginary start line on the water at full speed as the large clock moves from 0 seconds (at the 12 o’clock position) to 1.0 seconds. Athletes who achieve a start closer to 0 seconds tend to take control of the race.

Online Start

The online start involves all racers starting simultaneously from the starting line.

Boat Racing Betting Limits

The minimum betting amount is KRW 100, and the maximum is KRW 100,000.

Boat racing is a public service that returns all of its profits to society.

It is a public service project that returns all proceeds, after deducting customer refunds and statutory taxes, as well as all remaining revenue excluding event operation costs, to society through initiatives such as the National Sports Promotion Fund and local government financial support.

  • 72% of total bets are paid out as winnings to customers with correct predictions. 16% is allocated to taxes, including leisure tax, education tax, and agricultural and fisheries development tax, supporting both national and local finances.
  • 12%, after event costs are deducted, funds the National Sports Promotion Fund, arts and culture funds, and youth development projects, contributing to the wellbeing and happiness of citizens.

Beginning of Boat Racing for Fun

In Korea, the Act on Track Cycling and Boat Racing was enacted by the National Assembly in December 1991. After over four years of preparation, the nation’s first boat racing business was launched in June 2002 at Misa Motorboat Racing Park.

History of Boat Racing

In 1885, in Germany, a 1.5-horsepower gasoline engine was mounted on a boat to allow it to run. In Japan, the first motorboat was produced in 1911 by the Ishikawa Shipyard.

The First Motorboat Race

The first motorboat race was an international race held in the United Kingdom in 1904. The following year, in 1905, the 1st Gold Cup, considered the World Championship of Motorboat Racing, was held in the United States.

Boat Racing in Japan

In Japan, boating races officially began in April 1952 at Omura BOAT RACE stadium in Nagasaki Prefecture and have continued to be held since then.
The supervising authority for boat racing in Japan is the Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport, and Tourism. The races are organised by 46 entities, including local governments, while 19 motorboat race associations are responsible for operating the races. The nationwide federation of motorboat racing associations handles the training and management of athletes.
There are approximately 1,600 registered athletes, including men and women, and many fall within the 40 to 50-year-old age group.

The Beginning of Boat Racing in Korea

In Korea, the Act on Track Cycling and Boat Racing was enacted by the National Assembly in December 1991. After over four years of preparation, the nation’s first boat racing business was launched in June 2002 at Misa Motorboat Racing Park.
